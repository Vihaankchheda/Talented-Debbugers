Balpakram National Park

Balphakram National Park is a national park located in the South
Garo Hills of Meghalaya, India, at an altitude of about 910 m
(3,000 ft) close to the international border with Bangladesh.
It was inaugurated in December 1987 and provides habitat for
barking deer, Asian golden cat, Bengal tiger, marbled cat,
wild water buffalo, red panda and Indian elephant1. The park
is spread over an area of 220 km². Balpakram means “land of
the eternal wind” according to the myth of the Garo people.
The park is also part of the Garo Hills Conservation Area
(GHCA), which has been nominated by the Central government
of India as a World Heritage Site12. The best time to
visit Balpakram National Park is from late October to May,
when the weather is pleasant without rainfall. You can
reach the park from Shillong by road from Guwahati airport
to Tura and Baghmara. Entry fees will have to be paid at
the park’s gate.
