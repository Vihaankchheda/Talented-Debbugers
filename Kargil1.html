Kargil

Kargil is a city in the Indian-administered Ladakh
region of the disputed Kashmir area1. Here are
some key details about Kargil:

Location: Kargil is located at the confluence of
multiple river valleys: the Suru River valley to
the north and south, the Wakha Rong valley to the
southeast leading to Leh, and the Sod Valley to
the east leading to the Indus Valley near Batalik.

Administration: It is the joint capital of Ladakh,
an Indian-administered union territory, and also the
headquarters of the Kargil district1. The city is
administered by the Ladakh Autonomous Hill
Development Council.

Population: As of 2011, the population of Kargil was
16,3381.

Languages: The official languages are Urdu, Purgi,
Ladakhi, Brokskat, and English. Other languages
spoken include Shina and Balti.

Geography: Kargil is located 204 kilometres east
of Srinagar in Jammu and Kashmir, and 234 kilometres
to the west of Leh. It is on the bank of the Suru
River near its confluence with the Wakha Rong river.

Kargil has also been the site of border conflicts
between India and Pakistan, including the Kargil
War in May–July 1999.
