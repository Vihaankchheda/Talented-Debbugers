Umiam Lake

Umiam Lake, also known as Barapani Lake or Dam Sait, is
a man-made reservoir located in Shillong, the capital
of the north-eastern Indian state of Meghalaya. The
lake was created by constructing a dam across the Umiam
river in the early 1960s. The principal catchment area
of the lake and dam is spread over 220 square km.
The lake serves as a major tourist attraction for the
state of Meghalaya and is a popular destination for
water sport and adventure facilities. Tourists visit
this spot for kayaking, water cycling, scooting, and
boating. Apart from storing water for electricity
generation, the lake also provides numerous ecosystem
services at micro, meso and macro levels. Downstream
irrigation, fisheries, and drinking water cater to
local anthropogenic needs. However, due to the rising
population of Shillong upstream of the lake, the lake
is starting to become highly polluted. The rivers,
Wah Umkhrah and Wah Umshyrpi, have become drains in
Shillong city. They carry household and market sewage,
and industrial waste which drains into the lake1.
