Aru Valley

Aru Valley is a serene and enchanting tourist spot
located in the Anantnag District of Jammu and
Kashmir, India12. It is situated around
12 kilometers from Pahalgam. The valley is
nestled amidst green grasslands with a stunning
view of the majestic Himalayan ranges.

Aru Valley is noted for its scenic meadows and is
popular among tourists for its natural environment
and scenery1. It serves as a base camp for trekking
to the Kolahoi Glacier and Tarsar Lake. The
village lies on the left bank of the Aru river,
which is a tributary of the Lidder river.

During winters, when it’s covered with snow, Aru Valley
becomes a famous destination for skiing & heli-skiing.
Other tourist activities include fishing in the Lidder
river, trekking, hiking, horse riding, sightseeing,
and photography1.
