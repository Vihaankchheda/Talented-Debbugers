Cherapunji

Cherrapunji is regarded as one of the wettest places on earth as
the highest rainfall is foreseen during the year. Tourists from
around the world come here to see the Dain-Thlen, Kynrem, and
Nohkalikai waterfalls that are known as the popular waterfalls
of Cherrapunji. The town encompasses some of the best waterfalls
in Meghalaya.Major attractions here are Living-Root bridges,
Mawsmai cave, Seven sisters falls, Eco-park and a garden of caves.
As it is situated in a forest, it does not have the option of
luxury stay rather you will get resorts in Meghalaya and some
hotels in the lap of nature which will be a great fit to stay with
family and friends.

Cherrapunji, also known as Sohra, is a sub-
divisional town in the East Khasi Hills district of Meghalaya,
India. It is located on the Shillong Plateau, about 35 miles
southwest of Shillong, the state capital 1. Cherrapunji is known
for its heavy rainfall and waterfalls. It has often been credited
as being the wettest place on Earth, but for now nearby Mawsynram
currently holds that distinction 12. The town has an elevation
of 1,430 meters (4,690 feet) above sea level 1.
