Mawlynnong Village

Mawlynnong is a village in the East Khasi Hills district of
Meghalaya, India. It is known for its cleanliness and was
voted as Asia’s cleanest village by Discover India
magazine in 200312. The village has a population of
around 900 people and is home to about 95 households.
The literacy rate in Mawlynnong is 90%1. Agriculture is
the primary occupation of the local population, with
betel nut being the main crop. During summers, pineapples
and lychees are grown and exported to nearby regions.
The village propagates the concept of community-based
ecotourism and women empowerment is one of its main
features.
