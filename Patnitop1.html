Patnitop

Patnitop is a hill station located between Ramban Town
and Udhampur city in the Ramban district of Jammu
and Kashmir, India. It is situated on the
Jammu-Srinagar National Highway, 112 km from Jammu, on
the way from Udhampur to Srinagar1. The hill station
sits at an altitude of 2,024 m (6,640 ft) on a plateau
in the Shivalik belt of the Himalayas1. The river
Chenab flows in close proximity to this location.

Patnitop is known for its endless meadows and
panoramic views of the snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas.
It offers activities like skiing and trekking, and is
also famous for its water springs2. The hill station is
also known for its street shopping, with dry fruits,
Kashmiri handicrafts, and Pashmina shawls being popular.

One of the significant landmarks in Patnitop is the
Dr. Syama Prasad Mookerjee Tunnel1. At 9.2 km, it is India’s
longest road tunnel1. The tunnel starts from about 2 km from
Chenani town south of Patnitop to Nashri village north of
Patnitop, reducing the distance from Jammu to Srinagar by
31 km and bypassing Patnitop.
