Kupwara

Kupwara is a district in the Indian-administered Jammu
and Kashmir, located in the disputed Kashmir region.
It is one of the 10 districts in the Kashmir Valley
Division. The district is situated in the northern
frontier with an area of 2379 square kilometers,
having more than 240 km of Line of Control (LOC).

The district is exquisitely beautiful with vibrant
culture, diverse heritage, unique folklore, and vast
meadows. It is located between the Pir Panchal and Shams
Bari mountain ranges, which have beautiful meadows and
pastures providing grazing ground for cattle and sheep.
These mountain ranges also serve as health resorts and
tourist spots for adventurous souls.

The Pohru River, originating in the Lolab Valley and
flowing from east to west, and the Mawar River are two
main rivers in the district1. Both of them meet the
Jhelum River in the Baramulla district.
