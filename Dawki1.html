Dawki

The Dawki River, also known as the Umngot River and Wah Umngot,
is a river that flows through Dawki, a little town located at
the bottom of the Jaintia Hills in West Jaintia Hills district
in the Indian state of Meghalaya. It is one of the most
beautiful and clear rivers in Meghalaya, and is also known as
the ‘Magic River’. The river originates from the south-east
part of the state and flows northwards, passing through
several villages before it joins the Brahmaputra River in West
Garo Hills. The length of the river is about 160 kilometers.
Dawki River is a popular tourist destination and offers various
activities such as boating, kayaking, and fishing. The crystal
-clear water of the river is ideal for swimming and snorkeling.
The best time to visit Dawki is between October-April, i.e.,
the dry months. The river overflows during the rainy season.
If you visit just after the rainy season you can see the
waterfalls on the way to Dawki filled with water.
