Leh

Leh Explore Leh is a city in the Indian-administered Ladakh region,
located in the disputed Kashmir region1. It is the
largest city and the joint capital of Ladakh1. Leh,
located in the Leh district, was also the historical
capital of the Kingdom of Ladakh.

The city is situated near the eastern parts of Jammu
and Kashmir, on the crossroads of the historic “Silk Route”
from Sinkiang to West Asia and to the plains of India. The
city lies near the River Indus and is surrounded by the
Himalaya Mountains.

Leh is known for its vibrant culture, diverse heritage,
unique folklore, and vast meadows1. The city is home to
several ancient monasteries and structures, including the
Leh Palace, which was the former residence of the royal
family of Ladakh1. The palace was built in the same style
and about the same time as the Potala Palace in Tibet.
