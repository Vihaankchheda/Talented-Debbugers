Jammu City

Jammu is a city in the Indian-administered Jammu and Kashmir
region. It is the largest city and the winter capital of
the union territory12. The city is located in the
southwestern part of Jammu and Kashmir along the Tawi River,
south of Srinagar (the summer capital), and to the north
is the Siwalik Range.

Jammu is also known as the “City of Temples” as it has many
temples and shrines, with glittering shikhars soaring into
the sky, which dot the city’s skyline, creating the ambiance
of a holy and peaceful city.

The city lies near the eastern parts of Jammu and Kashmir, on
the crossroads of the historic “Silk Route” from Sinkiang to
West Asia and to the plains of India1. The city is connected
via National Highway 1 to the southwest and to Manali in the
south via the Leh-Manali Highway.
