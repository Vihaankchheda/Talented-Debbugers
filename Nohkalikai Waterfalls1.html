Nohkalikai Waterfalls

Nohkalikai Falls is the tallest plunge waterfall in India. It
is located near Cherrapunji, one of the wettest places on
Earth. The height of the waterfall is 340 metres (1,115 ft)
or 335 metres (1,100 ft) . The waterfall is fed by
rainwater collected on the summit of a small plateau and
decreases in power during the dry season from December to
February.
The name Nohkalikai is derived from a tragic legend of a
woman named Likai who jumped off the cliff next to the
falls after her husband killed and cooked their daughter
. The waterfall is open to visitors from 9:00 AM to
5:00 PM every day of the week. The entry fee for Indian
citizens is Rs. 20 per person and for foreign nationals,
it is Rs. 50 per person.If you are planning to visit
Nohkalikai Falls, you can also explore other nearby
attractions such as Mawsmai Cave, Seven Sisters Falls,
and Dainthlen Falls
